# Trading Bandarmology — Karo Karo Framework

A trader's application of bandarmology: shorter timeframes (days to a few weeks), tighter discipline, more focus on tradeable setups than on multi-month accumulation. Complements the investment-style bandarmology framework for those who want active engagement rather than position holding.

## Step 1: Timeframe and intent

This framework is for active trading, not investing:
- Holding period: 1 day to ~4 weeks
- Goal: capture the markup portion of bandar moves, not the full cycle
- Tolerate fewer drawdowns than the investment version; tighter stops
- More dependent on confirmation; less reliant on patience through ambiguity

## Step 2: Daily broker net-flow scan

The core daily routine is identifying stocks where bandar activity is unusually elevated:

- **Daily net buy concentration** — stocks where top 3 brokers account for an outsized share of net buying
- **Sudden net flow change** — stocks that went from neutral/distribution to strong net accumulation in recent sessions
- **Foreign vs. domestic divergence** — foreign brokers heavily net buying while domestic retail is neutral or selling (or vice versa)
- **Volume surge with concentrated buying** — abnormal volume day where the buying is concentrated, not distributed
- **Persistence over multiple days** — same broker net buyer pattern across 3–5+ sessions

Stocks that show up on these scans are candidates for further analysis. Most should be dropped after step 3.

## Step 3: Confirm the setup type

Categorize the candidate into a setup pattern:

**Early accumulation breakout candidate**
- Stock has been in a base; broker concentration intensifying
- Price holding above or testing prior support
- Volume increasing on up days
- Target: trade the breakout when it occurs

**Continuation in active markup**
- Stock already trending up
- Broker accumulation continues; same top buyers persistent
- Healthy pullbacks find support with reduced volume
- Target: enter pullbacks; ride the trend

**Late-stage caution**
- Stock has had extended markup
- Broker accumulation cooling; some prior buyers flipping to sellers
- Volume on advances diminishing; volume on declines rising
- Target: tighten stops; consider exit

**Failed accumulation**
- Broker concentration was present but price action has broken support
- Stop-loss territory; exit not entry

## Step 4: Entry rules

Discipline is the difference between bandar trading and bandar gambling:

- **Don't chase** — enter on pullbacks to support or on confirmed breakouts, not in the middle of an extended move
- **Confirm with volume** — entry on up days with above-average volume; avoid entering on low-volume drift
- **Confirm with broker flow** — top accumulators should still be net buyers on entry day or recent days
- **Sizing tiered to confirmation** — full position only on highest-confirmation setups (multiple supports aligned)

## Step 5: Stop discipline

- **Initial stop** — below the relevant structural support (range low for breakouts; prior pullback low for continuation)
- **Time stop** — if the setup doesn't work within 5–10 sessions, the thesis is questionable; reduce or exit
- **Broker-flow stop** — if the top accumulators flip to net sellers persistently, the setup has invalidated regardless of price
- **No averaging down** — wrong is wrong; add only on confirmed continuation

## Step 6: Profit-taking and exits

Trader-style exits, not investor-style:

- **Scale out at resistance** — partial profit at first major resistance; ride remainder
- **Trail the stop** — under each significant pullback's low
- **Exit on distribution signs** — buying climax with absorption, top accumulators flipping
- **Exit on broker concentration breakdown** — buying disperses across many brokers (often distribution being disguised)

## Step 7: Universe and risk management

Active bandar trading benefits from focus:
- **Pre-screened universe** — liquid mid-caps with sufficient broker activity for signals to be meaningful
- **Avoid micro-caps with thin trading** — broker concentration there reflects illiquidity, not bandar intent
- **Position count** — typically 5–10 concurrent positions; more dilutes attention
- **Total exposure caps** — sector and overall, to avoid hidden correlation

## Step 8: Realistic expectations

- Win rate around 50–60% on disciplined setups is realistic
- Average winner should exceed average loser meaningfully (1.5× or better) for the system to work
- Drawdowns of 10–15% on the trading account are normal
- Most months will be flat to slightly up; occasional strong months produce the year's return

## Output format

1. **Setup type** — early accumulation / continuation / late-stage / failed.
2. **Daily broker flow** — top net buyers and sellers, persistence, recent shift.
3. **Volume and price context** — what the tape is doing alongside broker data.
4. **Entry** — exact price zone and trigger.
5. **Stop** — price level, basis (structure / broker flow / time).
6. **Targets** — scale-out levels.
7. **Position size** — relative to standard, with rationale.
8. **What invalidates** — clean failure conditions.

## What to avoid

- Don't chase moves after they've extended. Patience beats FOMO.
- Don't average down. Ever.
- Don't ignore time stops. Setups that haven't worked in 10 sessions usually never do.
- Don't trade names where broker activity is just illiquidity. Real bandar setups have liquid underlying.
- Don't confuse a busy broker summary with directional intent. Concentration with persistence is the signal; sporadic activity is not.
